Pass Requirements

Understanding SA Pass Levels

Official DBE NSC pass requirements. You must pass at least 6 out of 7 subjects.

🎓 Bachelor's Pass

Minimum APS: 23+ | Pass 6/7 subjects
  • Home Language at least 40%
  • 4 subjects at 50%+ (high-credit)
  • LOLT / FAL at least 30%
  • 2 remaining subjects at 30%+
  • Life Orientation excluded from APS
  • Qualifies for Bachelor's degree

📜 Diploma Pass

Minimum APS: 19+ | Pass 6/7 subjects
  • Home Language at least 40%
  • 3 other subjects at 40%+
  • LOLT / FAL at least 30%
  • 2 remaining subjects at 30%+
  • Life Orientation excluded from APS
  • Qualifies for diploma programmes

📋 Higher Certificate

Minimum APS: 15+ | Pass 6/7 subjects
  • Home Language at least 40%
  • LOLT / FAL at least 30%
  • 2 other subjects at 40%+
  • 3 remaining subjects at 30%+
  • Life Orientation excluded from APS
  • Qualifies for certificate programmes

❌ Did Not Qualify

APS below 15 or fewer than 6 subjects passed
  • Does not meet NSC minimum requirements
  • Consider Matric Rewrite / upgrading
  • GHURU offers upgrading support (R400/mo)
  • Many learners succeed on their second attempt
  • Contact us for a personalised study plan
University Guide

APS Requirements for Major SA Universities

Minimum APS scores for general Bachelor's degree admission. Specific programmes may require higher scores.

🏛️ University of Pretoria (UP)

APS: 30+

Competitive entry. Strong focus on STEM and Commerce. Some programmes require 34+ APS. English and Mathematics often compulsory.

🏛️ University of Johannesburg (UJ)

APS: 26–30

Broader access programmes available. Foundation pathways for students with 20–25 APS. Strong Arts and Humanities faculty.

🏛️ University of the Witwatersrand (Wits)

APS: 34–42

Highly competitive. Health Sciences and Engineering require 38–42 APS. Strong research output and global reputation.

🏛️ University of Cape Town (UCT)

APS: 30–42

Top-ranked in Africa. Medicine and Actuarial Science require 42+ APS. Commerce and Humanities generally 30–34 APS.

🏛️ University of KwaZulu-Natal (UKZN)

APS: 28–38

Strong local option for KZN students. Medicine and Law competitive. Agriculture and Education more accessible at 28 APS.

🏛️ Stellenbosch University

APS: 30–40

Prestigious research university. Law and Engineering highly competitive. Afrikaans and English medium of instruction.
